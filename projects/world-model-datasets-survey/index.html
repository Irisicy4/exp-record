Interaction Datasets for Training World Models

Survey of widely used datasets, anchored on MineWorld (arXiv:2504.08388) · compiled 2026-07-09

Anchor paper. MineWorld: a Real-Time and Open-Source Interactive World Model on Minecraft (Microsoft Research, 2025) is a visual-action autoregressive Transformer trained on paired Minecraft game scenes and player actions; its training data comes from the openly released OpenAI VPT contractor dataset (row 1 below). This page surveys the other famous interaction datasets that the world-model literature trains on, grouped by domain.

Action labels: actions ground-truth or pseudo-labeled action sequences paired with observations · latent no recorded actions — the model infers latent actions from video · video-only passive video used for world-model pretraining · Access: open / proprietary

Takeaways

  • Three routes to “interaction”: record real actions (VPT, OXE, nuScenes) · let an agent generate its own data in a simulator (GameNGen, Atari) · infer latent actions from raw video (Genie) — the latent-action route is what unlocks internet-scale video.
  • Minecraft is the common testbed because VPT gives the largest open action-labeled corpus in any domain (~2.5k h exact mouse/keyboard); MineWorld, Oasis and Dreamer 4 all sit on top of it.
  • Scale is bimodal: action-labeled data tops out at thousands of hours (VPT 2.5k h, GAIA-1 4.7k h), while video-only corpora reach 30k–270k h — hence the standard recipe of video pretraining + small action-labeled fine-tuning (GenAD/Vista, V-JEPA 2 → V-JEPA 2-AC).
  • Data quality beats quantity: Genie’s curated 30k h (12% of the crawl) outperformed training on the full 244k h; DIAMOND simulates CS:GO from just 87 h.
